    Flight Buddy

    A feature to the Aertrip App
    JANUARY, 2020

    Planning and making sure you are ready to board a flight has shown to be an anxious experience for many flight-goers. The feature showcased below is an experience I worked on at Aertrip to aggregate all the needs of a traveller after they book a flight and keep them company up until they reach their destination.

    Inspiration

    BookMyShow in early 2019 released a feature called the Movie Mode which led us to extrapolate a similar experience for flight-goers. It seemed to fit right into something as complex/ intimading as air travel for a lot of users. It took the goal of the experience a step further than delight by guiding a user through a set a sequential tasks especially useful in the context of airports.

    User Personas and Journeys

    Realising user personas and analysing journeys took place somewhat simultaneously in the process.

    Since the opportunities identified during the user journeys varied based on whether they were travelling domestically or internationally, the users were categorised as domestic and international travellers.

    The user journey was divided into 7 phases.

    Trello cards were used to record and brainstorm user journeys. Cards were arranged under each phase chronologically. Each card was populated with a scenario/expectation which was replaced with opportunities to be included in the experience if any. Each card was also labelled to mark their respective personas or any other specific scenarios so that it can also be sorted.

    Ideation

    The experience structurally comprises of cards having time stamps relative to the user's departure time. Depending on when the user accesses the feature, it scrolls to the card appropriate for the user at that particular time. In order to identify the cards and their time stamps, user journey was mapped.

    Wireframing and a major change

    It was decided based on user interviews to distill the experience and limit it to the last 5 phases of the journey. The number of cards was further refined due to the lack of reliable APIs to display relevant data after discussions with developers.

    Final design

    A user is notified of the Flight Buddy feature on the homescreen of the app 24 hours prior to his flight. However, they can access the mode from his bookings as well. Clicking on them would reveal to them the Flight Buddy in a scrolled down state corresponding the time at which the user accessed it.

    The feature remains collapsible at any time if there is a need to navigate to other pages and could also be shared with friends and fellow travellers once they download the mobile app and register as users.

    Further work was focused on improving the user experience of each card
