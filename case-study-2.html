    Flight Buddy

    A post flight booking experience
    JANUARY, 2020

    Planning and making sure you are ready to board a flight has shown to be an anxious experience for many flight-goers. Beginning with what to pack for a trip, to the journey to the airport, to the myriad checkpoints within the airport, flight-goers ranging from casual to frequent flyers have several points during their journey where they require assistance. This mobile experience aims to be an aggregator of those identified needs after a person has booked a flight ticket.

    I worked on this mobile experience in the capacity of a UX Designer at Aertrip, an online travel portal.

    Inspiration

    BookMyShow in early 2019 released a feature called the Movie Mode which led us to extrapolate a similar experience for flight-goers. It seemed to fit right into something as complex/ intimading as air travel for a lot of users. It took the goal of the experience a step further than delight by guiding a user through a set a sequential tasks especially useful in the context of airports.

    User Types

    Opportunities in the user journeys varied primarily based on whether they were travelling domestically or internationally resulting in a continuum with domestic and international travellers in its extremities. Several other continuums such as frequency of travel, size of group travelling/ solo traveller, budget of the traveller etc. were also taken into consideration based on user interviews.

    User Journeys

    Realising user personas and analysing journeys took place somewhat simultaneously in the process.

    The user journey was divided into 7 phases.

    Out of the 7 phases, the last 5 phases were to be part of the experience to be designed.

    Trello was chosen to brainstorm the user journey. Each list was assigned a phase of the journey. Each card under a list was populated with a scenario / expectation (in uppercase) which was replaced with opportunities if any (in lowercase). Cards were arranged under each phase chronologically. Each card was also labelled to mark their respective personas or any other specific scenarios so that it can also be sorted.

    A few rounds of feasibility discussions based on availability of reliable APIs and implementation led us to cut down the final set of cards in the experience to a minimum viable product.

    Further Scope

    The experience provides a lot more opportunities than currently in the pipeline. The revamp to air travel post the Coronavirus outbreak, has also opened up quite a few opportunities within the experience.

    1. COVID-19 related guidelines and updates : At this juncture when guidelines related to travel are constantly changing resulting in a lot of confusion among travellers, Flight Buddy can become an effective facilitator for communication of updated protocols related to the user's journey.
    2. Empowering power users : Incorporating the "Where's my flight?" feature for frequent flyers or flight enthusiasts that gives the status and location of your flight at any time or creating custom packing lists have also emerged as interesting requirements for tech-savvy flight-goers.
    3. Fostering business partnerships : And finally, there also are opportunities for paid promotions of online streaming applications like Netflix or Amazon Prime and of lounges or stores within airports.

    With advancements in the infrastructure and policies surrounding air travel, 'Flight Buddy' is a feature that can evolve to educate and be a helping hand to flight-goers.
