    Openhouse after-school learning | Online classes and clubs

    A really cool product design experiment!
    SEP 2020 - MAR 2021

    What's the story? ðŸ“–

    Openhouse a.k.a. oh was an informal offline (until the pandemic) space for students to form clubs, attend workshops after school hours and for teachers to take tuitions. It aimed to redefine how the world learns and empower students to become thinkers and leaders.

    But when the pandemic hit, the vibrant offline space could no longer be where teachers and kids convened for classes and clubs. The classes migrated online. And they soon realised that they could not facilitate the kind of classes they had envisioned for their future on traditional video conferencing platforms.

    I was part of a very tight "single designer - single product manager working very closely with the founder" team entrusted to build an online learning space tailored towards teacher and student communities at oh for most of my time here.

    Openhouse was a very interesting business to unpack and learn from. It was a two sided market where parents and students made the demand side and teachers, the supply side. The business worked on an income sharing basis with teachers.

    User research and persona hypothesis

    Openhouse was a young team passionate about education wanting to make a dent in the way our future generations learn.

    Assumption

    Rote learning hinders the capacity of a student to apply concepts to situations around them. Hence, parents and students would invest in an alternate learning methodology.

    Insights from user interviews

    Our first task was to understand our customers and users and get a sense of what their thoughts were about several aspects around their education. We had access to about 50 students, their parents and a few teachers from oh's offline days. We interviewed 5 students, parents and teachers each to begin with. The general question we tried answering was:

    What makes learning experiences meaningful for students?

    Linked here is the semi-structured interview prepared for parents, students and teachers. We knew we had to be thinking about executing whatever we came up with for online classes as well, so the questions included their experience with online classes. The goal of the interview was to understand who our users typically are and what they considered a meaningful learning experience. And what you see below is data presented in a matrix by person and themes.

    To identify themes in the data, we used Affinity mapping that helped us formulate our learning philosophy, SHAPE (Social, Holistic, Active, Personalised and Empowered) :

    1. Social

      2. This is a big one for after-school learning. Students have a choice in who their tutors are unlike schools. And they chose to learn from people who they believe are credible, even friendly if that's a choice. Nurturing every relationship and interaction they have in our space is important to us.

      A decision that keeps with this learning philosophy that came easy was to maintain the tested and proven vibe at oh's offline space. This was a space students came to hang out with friends and attend variety workshops. Hence, the first steps were to simulate a similar social experience online.

    2. Holistic

      3. We believe that the ultimate goal of learning is not just academic success, but overall development of the learner. We pay attention to the emotional and mental well being of the learner.

      We took a very difficult decision to engage only with teachers that are friendly, empathetic, good facilitators, have a passion to learn new things/ have hobbies, adapt and be enthusiastic about contributing to a new methodology of learning.

    3. Active

      4. People learn when they are ready to learn, not when we are ready to teach them. We want the learner to be cognitively and emotionally engaged through all of the learning experiences we facilitate for them.

      We invested in experts from the field of education to develop new learning materials based on 'learning by doing' and train teachers on facilitation.

    4. Personlised

      5. Each of our learners have diverse skills, interests and needs. They have their own sense of identity and their beliefs are constantly evolving. Preserving them is important to us.

      We decided to provide avenues for kids to express themselves, their evolving identities and meet like-minded individuals. Treating everybody with compassion to nurture each individual lies at the core of our beliefs. We also track the participation and progress of every student and provide custom feedback on each student towards achieving their individual goals.

    5. Empowering

      6. We want the learner to take charge of their own learning and access doorways of opportunity. The purpose of learning is to build capacity to pick up any knowledge relevant to this fast paced world.

      We aim to connect students with a compassionate and talented community of peers and facilitators. Openhouse also organises workshops and has clubs that encourage students to go beyond academics and excel in anything that they desire.

    Persona hypothesis

    Enter Active learning and 'Experience sets'!

    Active learning focuses on teaching through projects and activities. After-school learning programs/ tuitions are common in India among students from grade 8th through 12th. Many complex foundational concepts that are not explored through projects and activities also lie in those grades. Hence, Openhouse decided to initiate a project to design "Experience Sets" based on the philosophy of Active Learning to facilitate lessons and concepts covered in the CBSE, ICSE and IB syllabi for these grades. A team of academicians that made up the Learning Design team was assigned this task.

    A quick brainstorming session was conducted with an extended team that aimed to deconstruct some of our memorable learning experiences, inside and outside of schools. We identified that every memorable learning experience we shared started off with feeling curious followed by some experimentation/ exploration and ended with some form of reflection. Our Learning Design Team attempted to do just that with an 'Experience set'.A few Experience sets were designed for different subjects and we tested them out in a couple of live classes.

    In a class of 6-8 students online, we found that the experience sets really engaged students. The user feedback on it was very promising when compared to how the same lesson would have been facilitated traditionally. Although the scalability of it was a concern, the Learning Design team decided it was worth the effort. The team incorporated existing learning protocols like quizzes, jeopardy, spin the wheel, memory palace etc. and continued to work on these Experience Sets.

    Next, Oh meets- the learning space

    "Even if the pandemic goes away, this can happen again and online classes are definitely going to be part of the contigency plans from now on. I think institutions would be gearing up to seamlessly transition to online classes in case of these situations again."

    The above reflection by a teacher justifies any effort taken in the direction of making online classrooms better.

    This project involved creating a delightful online learning space for oh student and teacher communities. After brainstorming and moodboarding a couple of metaphors for the learning space, we came up with the oh lobby for discovery of services that were to be offered.

    I worked on the design for the lobby.

    Synchronous online classes

    We then focused on the video conferencing platforms where synchronous learning takes place. All of the teachers we interviewed used either Zoom or Google Meets. We shadowed classes on Zoom and Google Meets at oh and conducted focus groups with teachers and students. The objective was to identify their pain points conducting and attending online classes. We looked at several interactions/ user tasks in a class from online and offline classes and create a priority list. We also found a very interesting competitor called 'Class for Zoom' that was trying to solve some critical problems for online synchronous classes.

    I worked with the project manager on creating the feature list and brainstorming the possibilities.
