    Openhouse after-school learning | Online classes and clubs

    A really cool product design experiment!
    SEP 2020 - MAR 2021

    What's the story? ðŸ“–

    Openhouse a.k.a, oh, before the pandemic, was a successful offline space for students of classes 6-12th to experience an alternate learning style and build student communities after school hours. It aims to redefine how the world learns and empower students to become thinkers and leaders.

    But when the pandemic hit, the vibrant offline space could no longer be where the teachers and kids convened for their classes and clubs. The classes migrated online. And they soon realised that they could not facilitate the kind of classes they had or envisioned for their future on traditional video conferencing platforms. That's when Openhouse decided to raise money and build an interactive video conferencing platform tailored towards their teacher and student communities.

    The Idea and Validation

    Openhouse was a very interesting business to unpack and learn from. One of the first things I learnt is that it is a two sided market where parents and students make the demand side and teachers, the supply side. The business works on an income sharing basis with teachers.

    The General Question

    How can we deliver a wholistic online learning experience to students?

    The 2 main assumptions in this general questions that need validation are:

    1. Rote learning hinders the capacity of a child to apply concepts to situations around them. Parents and students are looking for alternate learning styles.
    2. Existing online platforms are unable to facilitate an engaging classroom experience.

    Persona hypothesis

    Enter "Active Learning"!

    Although, there are cases to be made for rote learning, there is no denying the fact that active learning if practised at scale could prove tremendously useful for children. Hence, it was concluded that it is worth investing in augmenting these after-school classrooms with meaningful active learning exercises. Active learning focuses on teaching through projects and activities. The pedagogy is implemented at scale with the help of "Experience Sets" that makes for oh's secret sauce! This is developed and curated by a team of expert educators that make up the Learning Design team at oh.
